Professor Baaquie

The behaviour of the Dirac quantum field under time reversal is being studied; in particular, it has been shown that the multi-particle states yield a generalization of the Kramers degeneracy that is well known for spin systems in nonrelativistic quantum theory. Also being studied are the properties of the Dirac vacuum functional for encoding information, and the possible contributions of the vacuum to the entanglement of multi-particle states.

Dr. Cindy Ng

AstroPhysics and Cosmology.

Currently, many astronomical phenomena have been proposed as tests for cosmological models.
They include:
1. The brightness and redshift relations of Type Ia supernovae and gamma-ray burst,
2. The statistics of gravitational lensing of quasars,
3. The cosmic microwave background anisotropy.

I am interested to study these astronomical objects and their reliabilities to test for cosmological models. The cosmological model describes the origin of our universe and how we arrive, it also tells us the future evolution of our universe and how we will end up.

Dr Yeo Ye

Multiparticle entanglement.

My current research interest is in quantum entanglement associated with many parties. It lies at the heart of quantum information processing.

Entanglement is one of the most striking features of quantum mechanics, but it is also one of its most counterintuitive consequences of which we still have rather incomplete knowledge. Although the concentrated effort during the past decade has produced impressive progress, there is no general qualitative and quantitative theory of entanglement. It is hoped that we would be able to gain more understanding about the nature of multipartite entanglement by analyzing the roles of multipartite entangled states in various quantum information processes.
